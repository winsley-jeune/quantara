## Universal prerequisites

Required across every distributor application in every niche. Get these in place **before applying** to anyone:

| Item | Cost | Time | Where |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single-member LLC | $50–500 | 1–3 days | Your state's Secretary of State |
| EIN | Free | 10 min | irs.gov |
| State resale certificate | Free–$100 | Same-day (most states) | State Department of Revenue |
| Business bank account | Free | 1–3 days | Mercury, Relay, Chase Business |
| Business email at your domain | $6/mo | 1 hour | Google Workspace or Fastmail |
| Business phone (not cell) | Free | 10 min | Google Voice routed to cell |
| DUNS number (optional, but unlocks bigger accounts) | Free | 1–30 days | dnb.com/get-a-duns |
| Virtual mailbox (if no commercial address) | $10–20/mo | Same-day | Earth Class Mail, iPostal1 |

**Critical:** Use a business email at your own domain (`you@yourdomain.com`) — gmail/yahoo applications get rejected by serious credit teams (Kele, Trane, Ferguson). And expect a phone-verification call asking about your business — rehearse a 30-second pitch covering "what we sell, target customer, projected first-year volume."

## Niche 1 — HVAC sensors & controllers

Target brands: Belimo, Veris, Setra, Honeywell, Johnson Controls, Schneider, Siemens, ACI, plus challengers (BAPI, Greystone, KMC, Functional Devices, Mamac, Dwyer).

### The channel structure

Two-tier. Manufacturers don't sell to small online resellers — they sell to **stocking distributors**, who sell to contractors and resellers. The manufacturer "Authorized Distributor" tiers (e.g., Belimo Platinum: Control Depot, ICD, Rawson, Boston Aircontrols) are multi-year performance-rebate relationships, not 30-day onboarding. Apply at distributors, not manufacturers.

2. **HVAC USA** — explicit drop-ship, 2-business-day response, master distributor for Siemens/JCI/Belimo. Application sent to `sales@hvacusa.com`. **Apply Day 1 in parallel.**

Skip for now: Ferguson HVAC (license gate), Trane Supply (EPA cert + territory rep gate), KMC direct (integrator gate), all direct-manufacturer programs.

### MAP reality

**Belimo's MAP is the most important policy in this niche.** [Belimo MAP policy PDF](https://www.belimo.com/mam/americas/marketing_communication/brochures/belimo_minimum_advertised_pricing_policy.pdf) sets the advertised price floor at **80% of current published list** — you may not advertise more than 20% off list. Enforcement: warnings → suspended supply → termination. Distributor margin to a new account is ~15–25% off MSRP, leaving 5–15 points before shipping/CC fees.

**Honeywell, JCI, Siemens, Schneider/Veris, Setra** — all enforce MAP through distributor agreements. Verify per-SKU with your account rep.

**Looser MAP (your margin SKUs):** Functional Devices RIB relays, smaller Greystone sensors, ACI basic sensors, BAPI accessories. Build the storefront around these.

## Niche 2 — Mechanical pump seals

Target: aftermarket Bell & Gossett, Goulds, Aurora, Berkeley, Taco, Grundfos cross-reference seal kits + cartridge seals + o-ring kits.

### The channel structure

Older, relationship-driven, phone-first. Premium brands (John Crane, Chesterton, Flowserve) are locked behind regional authorized-distributor exclusivity — **non-starters in 30 days**. The realistic lane is **aftermarket OEM-equivalent**: U.S. Seal Mfg, Vulcan Seals, AST, PPC, SEPCO. They want new distributors, publish cross-ref data freely, have lightweight intake forms.

### Ranked top 5 to apply

1. **U.S. Seal Mfg** — apply Day 1 via [distributor inquiry form](https://www.ussealmfg.com/distributor-inquiry-form). Lowest friction. **The keystone resource: their free [Pump Manufacturers Cross-Reference PDF](https://www.ussealmfg.com/media/qv1d2qpd/us_seal_mfg_pump_manufacturers_cross-reference.pdf)** — maps B&G/Goulds/Aurora/Taco/Berkeley OEM part numbers to U.S. Seal SKUs. This is your SEO content moat.
2. **Vulcan Seals** — apply Day 1 via [become-distributor form](https://www.vulcanseals.com/resources/become-distributor). Best drop-ship infrastructure in the niche (web portal, same-day dispatch on 80K+ seal lines + 20K o-rings). **White-label permitted** (sell under your own brand). Gold + Platinum tiers reward growth. Free [cross-reference tool](https://www.vulcanseals.com/resources/seal-cross-reference-tool-v3) and [American Coding System](https://www.vulcanseals.com/resources/american-coding-system) for SEO.
3. **Motion Industries** — apply Day 2 at a local branch with EIN + resale cert. Day-1 CC account; drop-ship explicitly supported; covers gaps in manufacturer catalogs. Margins are thin but breadth is unmatched (550+ branches).
4. **SealSales.com affiliate** — apply Day 2 as parallel cashflow backstop. Affiliate is fastest-yes; margins thinner but time-to-cash unbeatable while manufacturer apps mature.
5. **AST Seals** — apply Day 3 by **phone** (877-278-7325). 94% same-day shipping. Quality OEM-equivalent line.

Backups for Week 3 if any stall: SEPCO, PPC, Roten, Seal Distributors (Sanford FL).

### MAP reality

- **U.S. Seal, Vulcan, AST, PPC**: effectively **no MAP** at first-tier distributor level. Compete on price. SupplyHouse, Inline Sales, Boiler Supplies all sell same SKUs side-by-side at different prices.
- **John Crane, Chesterton, SEPCO**: strict MAP, monitored, dealership termination on violation.
- **Master MRO (Motion, Applied)**: pass-through whatever the brand enforces.

### The SEO play

Build `/cross-reference/[oem-part]` pages for every B&G/Goulds/Aurora seal kit number that appears in the U.S. Seal PDF (a few thousand SKUs). Each page becomes a low-competition long-tail keyword target. Premium brand cross-ref data (John Crane, Chesterton) is gated behind authorized-distributor portals — don't bother in Phase 1.

**Goldmine niche:** discontinued OEM parts. B&G/Goulds/Aurora EOL old pump models but the installed base runs 30–40 years. Aftermarket cross-ref kits (U.S. Seal, Vulcan, PPC) target exactly these orphaned pumps. SEO traffic on "B&G [discontinued model] seal kit replacement" converts at multiples of new-pump-seal traffic.

## Niche 3 — Restaurant equipment parts

Target brands: Hobart, Vulcan, True/Beverage-Air, Frymaster, Garland, Imperial, Pitco, Bunn.

### The structural truth — Parts Town moat confirmed

[Parts Town acquired Heritage in 2020, finalized integration Sept 2024](https://www.partstown.com/about-us/parts-town-completes-combination-with-heritage-foodservice-group). They run a [marketplace](https://www.partstown.com/marketplace-sellers) inviting sellers to list on *their* site — there is no "buy from us at wholesale and resell on your own site" program. Treat Parts Town as a competitor, not a supplier.

To match Parts Town's same-day-until-9pm-ET shipping, layer **GP-PartsDirect** (4pm CT cutoff) + **AllPoints FPS** (same-day from four regional DCs). Together they cover every US metro within 2 days.

- **Hobart, Vulcan, True**: actively monitor MAP. Amazon/eBay listings get reported within weeks; dealer agreements often terminate on a single violation.
- **Bunn, Pitco**: less strict.

**Standard MAP workaround for your own site:** "call for quote" or "add-to-cart to see price." Don't list OEM SKUs publicly below MAP.

- **Hobart / Vulcan / Wolf** (ITW Food Equipment Group): Authorized Service Agent network is territorial. New pure-online reseller approval = hard. Realistic path: buy through GP-PartsDirect (Vulcan MASC) + AllPoints.

- **Pitco**: [pitco.com/parts](https://www.pitco.com/parts/), ASAP program. Direct dealer setup possible for committed online sellers.
- **Bunn**: [Authorized Service Provider via Bunn Online Learning Center](https://commercial.bunn.com/support/service-tech-certification). Tech certification recommended.
- **Frymaster, Imperial, Garland, Lang, Cleveland Range, Wells** (Welbilt/Ali Group/Middleby): all source through GP-PartsDirect / AllPoints in Phase 1.

### Industry credibility

- **NAFED** (National Association of Food Equipment Dealers): $500–1500/yr — gives you trade-reference letterhead and onboarding credibility.
- **CFESA** (Commercial Food Equipment Service Association): distributors check this as credibility signal. Company-level certification requires 75% of business in parts/service — not a barrier to buying, but mentioning a CFESA-certified tech on staff (even contract) helps Net-30 approval.
- **SEFA** (Supply & Equipment Foodservice Alliance): buying group for dealers.

## Cross-niche gotchas

### Account setup
- **Business email at your domain is non-negotiable.** Gmail rejected by Kele, Trane, Ferguson, etc.
- **Business phone (not cell).** Distributors call to verify. Google Voice tied to business domain is fine.
- **Resale certificate ≠ tax-exempt certificate.** Resale = "I buy to resell." Tax-exempt = nonprofits/government. You need the **resale certificate** from your state's DOR. The [Multistate Tax Commission Uniform Resale Certificate](https://www.mtc.gov/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Unif-Resale-Cert-revised-10-14-22.pdf) is accepted by 36 states; FL, CA, NY do NOT accept out-of-state certs.
- **EIN is mandatory and free.** 10 min at irs.gov.
- **DUNS number is free.** 1–2 day standard issuance, up to 30 days worst case. Don't pay $229 for expedited.
- **Trade references for new LLCs**: substitute with (a) personal CC with $1K+ available, (b) bank reference (your business checking; tell your banker they may get a call), (c) any prior B2B relationship (hosting provider, accounting software).
- **Business bank account before applying.** Net-30 teams will not extend terms against a personal account. Mercury or Relay are free + same-week; Chase Business takes a branch visit.
- **PO box addresses**: accepted by some distributors (Blackhawk, HVAC USA per public terms), rejected by others (Ferguson, Trane want a physical commercial address). Earth Class Mail / iPostal1 virtual mailbox with real street address solves it.
- **Phone verification is universal.** Expect a 5-min call asking: how long in business, what you sell, trade references. Rehearse a 30-sec pitch.

### Drop-ship logistics
- **Blind shipping is rarely default — always negotiate per-order.** Manufacturers ship to YOUR address by default. "Blind drop-ship" (no manufacturer markings, your packing slip) requires explicit ask until you're trusted.
- **Same-day shipping cutoffs matter.** GP-PartsDirect 4pm CT; AllPoints 4 DCs each with different cutoffs; HVAC USA depends on inventory location. Build your customer-facing copy around "ships today from regional warehouse" — true, customer cares about delivery date not brand.

### Pricing & MAP
- **MAP enforcement is brand-by-brand, not distributor-by-distributor.** Distributor agreements just pass through manufacturer MAP. Belimo, True, Hobart, Vulcan: strict. Functional Devices, Greystone, Bunn, Pitco: looser. Private-label (Mavrik): none.
- **Online sales policy: ask explicitly, get in writing.** Most distributors won't write "yes you can sell on Amazon/eBay" because that's where MAP violations get caught by manufacturer enforcement teams. **Your own storefront at your own domain is universally permitted** if you respect MAP.
- **Realistic margin reality:**
  - Manufacturer-direct (U.S. Seal, Vulcan, AST, PPC, Mavrik): 30–45% off list
  - Master MRO distributor (Motion, Applied, Grainger): 5–15% off list — fine for breadth, kills margin
  - Secondary distributor (SealSales, Seal Distributors): 15–25% (you're 3rd tier)
  - HVAC controls Belimo/JCI/Honeywell new account: 15–25% off MSRP minus 80%-of-list MAP floor = 5–15 points gross
- **Plan SKU mix accordingly.** Anchor margin on no-MAP / private-label SKUs; treat MAP-policed brands as catalog credibility, not margin.

### Returns & warranty
- **Manufacturer warranty stays with the manufacturer.** You almost never own warranty as a reseller; customer registers with the brand, distributor handles RMA back to the manufacturer. Build customer-service flow to forward warranty claims to distributor and stay out of the loop financially.
- **Return shipping**: non-defective returns = buyer pays (you), per most distributor terms (Blackhawk: 30% restock + non-refundable shipping). Defective: distributor or manufacturer pays.
- **Refrigeration compressor warranties**: 5–7 years standard, but claim goes manufacturer → installing service agent → end customer. Your role: ship the part, forward the call.
- **Health-code "broken on arrival" returns**: industry default = reseller eats it unless manufacturer defect proven with photos within 48 hours. AllPoints/Mavrik's 90-day no-questions return is the soft cushion.

### Tech-support burden
- **Customers will call asking installation questions you can't answer.** Mitigate up front. Product pages: model-number lookup, exploded-parts diagrams, "find a CFESA tech near you" widget. Refuse install-advice calls politely — redirect to local service.
- **Your support workflow is "what part fits my model"** not "how do I install."

### Freight / oversize
- LTL Class 85–125 items (oven doors, fryer pots, mixer bowls): residential delivery surcharges $100+, liftgate fees $75+. Bake $50 freight markup into items >50 lb, or restrict to UPS-shippable parts in v1.

## 30-day execution checklist

### Days 1–3 — Foundation ($200)
- Form LLC (or confirm existing); get EIN at irs.gov
- Open business bank account (Mercury / Relay / Chase)
- Get state resale certificate (free from state DOR)
- Register domain + business email (`you@yourdomain.com`)
- Register free DUNS number at dnb.com
- Set up Google Voice business line
- Get virtual mailbox if no commercial address (~$10/mo)

### Days 3–7 — Applications (parallel)

**HVAC controls path:**
- Apply to Blackhawk Supply (download credit app, email to cs@blackhawksupply.com)
- Apply to HVAC USA (email sales@hvacusa.com)
- Apply to Jackson Systems TradesElite (free signup)

**Pump seals path:**
- Apply to U.S. Seal Mfg ([inquiry form](https://www.ussealmfg.com/distributor-inquiry-form))
- Apply to Vulcan Seals ([become-distributor](https://www.vulcanseals.com/resources/become-distributor))
- Walk into local Motion Industries branch with EIN + resale cert

**Restaurant parts path:**
- Self-register at GP-PartsDirect; request Net-10 credit app
- Apply to AllPoints FPS (phone 1-800-332-2500)
- Free signup at PartsFPS

### Days 7–14 — First orders
- First credit-card test order from whichever approves first
- Ship to a friend's address — verify drop-ship flow + packaging
- Use first approval as trade reference for next round of applications
- Apply to second-tier distributors (MSC ResaleLink, AllPoints if not yet, etc.)

### Days 14–21 — Build the site
- Skeleton Next.js + Vercel
- Reverse-engineer 5 competitor product-page structures
- Build catalog around no-MAP / private-label / looser-MAP SKUs first:
  - HVAC: Functional Devices RIB relays, Greystone sensors, ACI basics
  - Pump seals: anything from U.S. Seal + Vulcan cross-ref
  - Restaurant: Mavrik OCM line, Bunn parts
- Hand-write 20–30 part-number pages with: tech specs, wiring/cross-ref data, FAQ, JSON-LD Product schema

### Days 21–30 — Launch
- Submit sitemap to Google Search Console
- Build the next 30 part-number pages
- Drop-ship first 5–10 real orders manually
- Capture warranty registration follow-up for each customer
- Helpful technical answers on relevant forums (r/HVAC, Eng-Tips Mechanical Seals, ServiceTalk) — be useful, no spam
- Use resulting invoices as trade history for next round (Kele, alpscontrols, Schedule73, True TOPS direct)

### Realistic trajectory (estimated, not measured)
- Month 1–2: $0 (foundation + indexing)
- Month 3: $300–$2K (first sales as long-tail traffic starts)
- Month 6: $3K–$12K
- Month 12: $15K–$50K
- Month 18: $40K–$80K plausible at scale
