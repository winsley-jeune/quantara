## Niche A — Restaurant equipment parts

### Market size

- US foodservice equipment & supplies industry 2025: **~$14.5B**
- Parts aftermarket subset (estimated): **$3-4B/yr**
- IBISWorld 2025: commercial cooking equipment manufacturing $8.4B, slowing growth (8% → 2.3% CAGR through 2030)

### Competitor traffic (Similarweb April 2026)

| Site | Monthly visits | Organic % | Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| webstaurantstore.com | 12.7M | 86% US | General supplies |
| **partstown.com** | **1.3M** | 48.8% organic | The moat |
| reliableparts.com | 203K | 62% organic | Strong on appliances |
| allpointsfps.com | 91K | Direct-heavy | B2B-skewed |
| partsfps.com | 32K | 86% organic | Pure SEO play |
| generalparts.com (GP-PartsDirect) | **8.7K** | mostly display | **Functionally dormant for retail** |

### The Parts Town problem

- $650M annual revenue
- 1.3M monthly visits, 48.8% organic = ~635K organic visits/month
- Interactive parts-diagram pages that capture upstream "[model] parts diagram" searches
- Same-day shipping until 9pm ET
- Berkshire Partners capital backing same-day infrastructure
- Already optimized SERP — they own the part-number queries

**Estimated beatable share of page 1: 20-30%**. The "ugly old PHP page" archetype is rare — Parts Town has already invested in clean UX + schema.

### Per-query revenue math

- 100 searches/mo × 20% CTR × 10% conversion × $80 AOV = **~$160/mo per long-tail page**
- 5,000 indexed SKU pages all ranked → theoretical $5-17M/yr
- Realistic capture vs Parts Town incumbency: **15-25% = $750K-$4M/yr** (and only if you stock or drop-ship reliably)

### Anti-patterns

1. **OEM gatekeeping.** ITW (Hobart/Vulcan/Wolf) and Manitowoc have authorized-dealer programs. Selling genuine OEM without authorization invites legal letters + MAP enforcement.
2. **Same-day shipping is table stakes, not SEO.** Friday 4pm broken-fryer urgency wins on logistics, not rankings.
3. **Parts Town owns interactive-diagram UX.** Their diagram pages capture upstream queries you can't easily match (OEM-permission dependent).
4. **Webstaurant + Amazon compress head terms.** Long-tail is what's left, and Parts Town has it.
5. **Market is consolidating, not fragmenting.** Parts Town + Heritage (2019-2024 integration). New entrant arrives mid-consolidation, not pre-.

## Niche B — Mechanical pump seals

### Market size

- Global mechanical seals market 2026: ~$7.4B → $10.4B by 2033
- North America share ~32% = ~$2.4B regional
- US share: ~$1.7B total mechanical seals
- **Aftermarket / retrofit is ~60% of regional business = ~$1B US aftermarket**
- **Cartridge seals = 38% of product mix = ~$380M sub-segment**
- Online share: **extremely immature, ~10-15%**

### Competitor traffic (Similarweb April 2026)

| Site | Monthly visits | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| supplyhouse.com | 3.4M | Broad MRO; partial B&G overlap |
| boilersupplies.com | 6.9K | 80% organic share of keyword traffic |
| **vulcanseals.com** | **4.7K** | Brand site, weak SEO ingress |
| **ussealmfg.com** | **2.9K** | 73 total keywords, 100% organic |
| **sealsales.com** | **1.6K** | **43 total keywords**, 100% organic |
| astseals.com, ppcmechanicalseals.com, gaddismechanicalseals.com | <1K each | Functionally invisible online |

### Why this is the opening

**There is no Parts Town of pump seals.** Combined visits to all pure-play seal sites: under 15K/month. The "incumbents" rank for 43-73 keywords total. Vulcan Seals' own marketing pushes their cross-reference tool as the value prop — and they're not winning their own keyword.

**SERP composition on long-tail queries:**
- 1996-era HTML pages with no schema
- PDF service manuals ranking by accident
- eBay listings
- Amazon non-OEM aftermarket
- Dealer "request a quote" forms with no price, no buy button

**Estimated beatable share of page 1: 60-80%**. The SEO thesis ("clean Next.js storefront beats catalog shops") **is literally true here**.

### Per-query revenue math

- 100 searches/mo × 28% CTR (cleaner SERP) × 12% conversion (urgency-driven) × $200 AOV (cartridge seals) = **~$670/mo per long-tail page**
- 2,000 indexed SKU/cross-reference pages all ranked → theoretical $5.8-28M/yr
- Realistic capture vs weak incumbents: **40-60% = $2.3-17M/yr**

**Per-query economics are 3-5× better than restaurant parts:**
- Higher AOV (cartridge seals trade at $150-400 vs gaskets at $40-120)
- Less competition compresses top-3 CTR upward
- Downtime-driven conversion is higher (pump down = production down)

### Customer pain signals

- **Practical Machinist** ("Obsolete Victor oil seals cross reference"): *"old-line companies that made seals... have been merged or globalized, with only popular product lines kept under new names with new part numbers."* **Cross-reference is the job.**
- **MyTractorForum** threads: techs measuring OD/ID by hand and walking into bearing stores because they can't resolve cross-references online.
- **Heating Help forum** (Taco 007 thread): pricing intransparency — *"by the time you find the cartridge, a full pump is the same price."*
- **Eng-Tips Mechanical Seal Engineering**: hydraulic-seal selection done from manufacturer PDFs and hand-sourcing.
- **Vulcan Seals' own marketing** promotes a cross-reference tool as the value prop — they know it's the SEO gateway and they're not winning it.

### Anti-patterns

1. **Cross-reference data is the moat, and it's proprietary.** John Crane, Burgmann, Flexaseal, Pac-Seal, US Seal codes ↔ part numbers ↔ pump models exist as scattered PDFs. Curating this database is the unglamorous core of the moat — under-invest and you're just another thin storefront.
2. **B2B procurement runs on POs + Net-30**, not Stripe checkout. Industrial buyers (water utilities, refineries, food plants) often can't buy retail. Need account-based purchasing, tax exemption handling, possibly EDI for bigger accounts.
3. **Inventory carry vs drop-ship trade-off is harsh.** SKU-heavy, slow-turning. Stocking 2,000 SKUs ties up serious working capital; drop-shipping makes you dependent on your supplier's logistics.
4. **API 682 / hazardous-fluid certifications create technical liability.** Selling a wrong seal for an oil-and-gas API 682 application is not the same as shipping a wrong fryer thermocouple. Spec-fitment errors carry recall + liability risk.
5. **OEM B&G / Goulds / ITT can revoke authorization** or push aftermarket-equivalence disclosure rules. Your moat has to be aftermarket cross-reference, not OEM exclusivity.

## The verdict

**Pump seals.** The data is clear.

| Reason | Restaurant Parts | Pump Seals |
|---|---|---|
| Competitive moat | Captured by Parts Town | **No incumbent** |
| SERP beatability | 20-30% of long-tail | **60-80%** of long-tail |
| Per-query revenue | $80-280/mo | **$240-1,200/mo** |
| Realistic 5,000-page capture | $750K-$4M/yr | $2.3-17M/yr (smaller catalog, higher per-page) |
| Market timing | Mid-consolidation, late | **Pre-consolidation, early** |

The "ugly old PHP page" thesis that drew you to restaurant parts is actually MORE true in pump seals — restaurant parts have already been cleaned up by Parts Town's $650M war chest. Pump seals are where the SEO opening still exists.

## What you'd actually build

A pump-seal e-commerce site with:

1. **A cross-reference database as the core asset.** Every B&G/Goulds/Aurora/Taco/Grundfos OEM seal part number → US Seal / Vulcan / AST aftermarket equivalent. The U.S. Seal Pump Manufacturers Cross-Reference PDF + Vulcan's coding system are the raw material. Each cross-ref entry = a dedicated `/cross-reference/[oem-part]` landing page on your Next.js site.

2. **Drop-ship sourcing** from U.S. Seal Mfg + Vulcan Seals (both accept solo founders, both have public application forms). Motion Industries as breadth backup. SealSales.com affiliate as cashflow backstop.

3. **No retail trying** to fight head terms. Long-tail SKU pages only. Plus discontinued / obsolete pump model pages where SERP is empty — the "B&G [discontinued model] seal kit replacement" goldmine.

4. **B2B-friendly checkout**: tax-exempt upload, PO/Net-30 option (gated to verified businesses), bulk-quote forms. Stripe + Mercury banking handles 95% of the transactional side; the rest is form-based.
